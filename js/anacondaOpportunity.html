The Anaconda Opportunity.

Myself, Jonathan and Spencer spent two weeks hiking through what are some of the grandest locations in the world. We spent 3 days in the High Uintas Wilderness in Utah, including summiting Kings Peak, the highest point in Utah at 13,527' (4,123m) Then a brief two-night stop in Grand Teton National park which included doing the Paintbrush Divide loop. We then spent six days in the backcountry of Glacier National Park. Many altitude, such walking. Wow.
But first, we start in Utah.

Utah & Kings Peak

Our primary goal here was to summit Kings peak. Yet, in contrast to other times I've summited the mountain, on this fifth time for me, we wouldn't do it in one day nor only in 28 hours. We would take three days. This was to help those of us coming from lower altitude to aclimate, as well as to take in the beauty of the area, such that I now realize demands enough time to afford really taking it in.
